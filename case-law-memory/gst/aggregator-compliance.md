## Facts Pattern

**This research applies when:**
- Entity operates as a cab/ride aggregator platform
- Bookings are made through an electronic platform (app/website)
- Operations span multiple states
- Entity holds GSTIN in only one state but facilitates rides in others

---

## Legal Position

### 1. E-Commerce Operator as Designated Taxpayer

**Section 9(5) of the CGST Act, 2017** ✓ VERIFIED

The government may specify categories of services where the e-commerce operator (ECO) shall be liable to pay tax as if the ECO is the supplier of such services. Cab services booked through an electronic platform are notified under this provision.

**Effect:** Cabbazaar (as the aggregator) is the person liable to pay GST on rides booked through its platform — NOT the individual drivers.

### 2. Mandatory Registration — No Threshold Exemption

**Section 24(x) of the CGST Act, 2017** ✓ VERIFIED

"Every electronic commerce operator" is required to obtain compulsory registration regardless of aggregate turnover.

**Effect:** The usual ₹20L/₹40L threshold under Section 22 does NOT apply. Registration is mandatory from day one.

### 3. State-wise Registration Requirement

**Section 25(1) of the CGST Act, 2017** ✓ VERIFIED

"Every person who is liable to be registered... shall apply for registration in every such State or Union Territory in which he is so liable."

Read with **Section 12(9) of the IGST Act, 2017** ✓ VERIFIED — Place of supply of passenger transportation service is the place where the passenger boards the conveyance.

**Combined effect:** A Delhi GSTIN cannot cover rides originating in Maharashtra, Karnataka, etc. Separate registrations are required in EACH state where passengers board.

### 4. Recent Development — Section 9(5) Amendment

**Notification No. 17/2025-Central Tax dated 17.09.2025** ⚡ HIGH CONFIDENCE

Amended Section 9(5) provisions regarding ECO liability for "local delivery" services. Requires ECOs to pay 18% GST on local delivery services unless service provider is registered under Section 22(1).

**Relevance:** While primarily targeting delivery services, this notification reinforces the regime of ECO-as-taxpayer and state-wise compliance.

## Risk Assessment (for non-compliance)

| Risk | Provision | Consequence | Severity |
|------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Operating without registration in a state | Section 122(1)(xi) CGST | Penalty: higher of ₹10,000 or tax evaded | HIGH |
| Tax deposited in wrong jurisdiction | Section 77 CGST | Refund + re-deposit + interest u/s 50 (18% p.a.) | HIGH |
| No registration despite mandatory requirement | Section 122(1)(xi) read with Section 24 | Penalty + entire period's tax demand with interest | CRITICAL |
| Continued non-compliance post notice | Section 132 CGST | Prosecution for tax evasion if amount >₹5Cr | CRITICAL |

## Recommended Compliance Pathway

1. **Immediate:** Obtain GST registration in ALL states where operations are live/planned
2. **Timeline:** 7-10 working days per state (online application via GST portal)
3. **Filing:** Separate GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B for each GSTIN
4. **TCS:** Section 52 compliance — collect TCS and file GSTR-8 (if applicable to model)
5. **Ongoing:** Monitor new state launches → register BEFORE operations begin
